## Target Market

- Enterprise clients with white-label needs
- Target customer LTV: $5,000 - $50,000

## Key Pricing Psychology Principles

### Anchoring Effect
The first price a customer sees becomes their reference point. By showing the Enterprise tier first or emphasizing annual pricing, you anchor expectations higher.

### Decoy Pricing
Introducing a middle tier that makes the premium tier look more attractive. For example:
- Starter: $49 (basic features)
- Pro: $149 (best value - most popular)
- Enterprise: $499 (all features + support)

The Pro tier is designed to be the "no-brainer" choice.

### Value Metrics
Best SaaS pricing aligns with customer value:
- Usage-based: Per conversation, per minute
- Tiered: Feature gates at each level
- Hybrid: Base fee + usage overage

### Conversion Optimization
- 14-day free trial converts 15-25% (industry average)
- Freemium converts 2-5% but increases top-of-funnel
- Annual discounts (20-25%) improve cash flow and retention
- Transparent pricing increases conversion by 30%

## Revenue Projection Framework

### Customer Acquisition Assumptions
- Year 1: 100 customers (product-market fit phase)
- Year 2: 1,000 customers (growth phase)
- Year 3: 10,000 customers (scale phase)

### Tier Distribution
- Starter: 40% of customers (price-sensitive, low volume)
- Pro: 50% of customers (sweet spot, best margin)
- Enterprise: 10% of customers (high-touch, customization)

### Churn Assumptions
- Starter tier: 8-10% monthly churn
- Pro tier: 4-6% monthly churn
- Enterprise: 2-3% monthly churn

### Gross Margin Targets
- Target: 75-85% gross margin (SaaS standard)
- Contribution margin per customer:
  - Starter: 70% (lower margin due to support costs)
  - Pro: 82% (optimal efficiency)
  - Enterprise: 78% (higher support but premium pricing)
